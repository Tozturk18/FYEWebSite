BRI vs. The Silk Road

By Mei Ling, Kyle, Eric, Theo, and Tuna

Introduction:

The Belt and Road Initiative:

The belt road is a trillion dollar Chinese project started in 2013 and projected to be complete by 2049. It aims to improve land and sea trade across Asia, Europe, and Africa, as well as promote partnership among countries. It is meant to be a modern day reflection of the ancient Silk Road.

The Silk Road:

The Silk Road is an ancient trade route that spanned across Eurasia. Technology, ideologies, and goods were able to travel further than ever before. Many modern day cultural traditions owe its existence to The Silk Road’s success.

China-Central Asia West Asia Economic Corridor:

The Belt and Road Initiative is composed of multiple Economic Corridors. China-Central Asia West Asia Economic Corridor is one of the most important of those corridors. This region is home to 4% of World's total Natural Resources which makes it especially important for China and other world powers such as Russia and USA.However, this region brings more to China then just natural resources. This region acts as another passageway to Europe using the underground railroad tunnels in Istanbul, Turkey. this region is also geopolitacally valuable. It is close to Europe, Russia and China, which makes it a pivot point. China uses large amounts of investments to put heavy debt burden on countries. These debts are so big that they some times are bigger than 50% of the country's total GDP. This makes it impossible for these countries to pay back to China using money. This gives an opportunity to China to barter lands for repayment of debts.

China-Pakistan Economic Corridor:

China-Pakistan Economic Corridor is the most important to BRI, but also one of the most challenging to traverse. This corridor provides China with easy access to Indian Ocean and to their military bases in Africa. This Corridor also alows China to grow and develop its westernmost regions. These BRI corridors helps China to have better relations with other countries and have more trade partners. However, this reagion is one of the most challenging regions. On January 4 2010 a massive landslide occured blocking the river and causing waters to rise. This turned the river into 2.5 km wide 100 meters deep river. Moreover, one of the biggest effects of BRI is improving China's relations with other countries and increasing China's influence to overseas and even reaching to Europe.

How we did it:

Initially, using US Geological Survey Website, we collected 16bit Shuttle Radar Topography Mission (SRTM) data. However, we weren't able to select a region and take the image of the whole region, and because the images were 16 bit, we were not able to priview them. Hence using softwares to convert the images into 8 bit images and then combining them according to their Geo-locations. This allowed us to get images like the one on the right. After collecting these images, using Blender we were able to turn those images into 3D objects. However, these object were still not compatible for 3D printing because they lacked a base. Next step was to design for 3D printing for this we used Fusion 360 and imported the file from blender. Unfortunately the file was not compitable with Fusion to edit so we had to convert the mesh object into T-spline body. This allowed us to make sketches on it and edit the object. Finally we created a base for it to stand on and send the file to a CAM software. Using Prusa Slicer we imported the file and adjusted 3D printing settings according to that. Last but not least, we sent the file to the 3D printer and it took around 13 hours to print out one map. Finally after printing, using paint we marked the routes and make a map key on adobe Photoshop which we then glued on the map's flat part.

Regions:

Kashgar:

Kashgar is a Chinese Oasis city at the foot of the Pamir mountains in the west of the Tarim Basin. The city welcomed many traders and travelers from The Silk Road who would trade amongst themselves and the people of Kashgar.

Pamir Mountains:

The Pamir Mountains are a mountain range in Central Asia which The Silk Road poses through. The terrain of the Pamir Mountains are incredibly difficult to navigate and the temperature is cold year round. Today it is the home of the world’s most dangerous and second highest highway.
